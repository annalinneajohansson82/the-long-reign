# Purpose

This document defines the shared vocabulary of The Long Reign. All documents in the project must use these terms consistently. Definitions exist in exactly one place — this glossary (or expanded entries in `docs/glossary/`).

---

# Overview

The glossary is the project's dictionary. If a term is used in any document and has a specific meaning within the project, it must be defined here.

Expanded glossary entries with additional detail may be stored in `docs/glossary/` as individual files — one per term. This document serves as the canonical index.

---

# Core Terms

### Capital

The player's primary and permanent settlement. There is exactly one capital. It grows from a camp into a thriving kingdom over the course of the game. The capital cannot be moved or replaced.

**Source**: Handoff, Colonies section

---

### Chronicle

One of the game's flagship systems. An automatically maintained historical record that tracks major events: births, marriages, deaths, colony foundings, discoveries, disasters, hero achievements, completed wonders, wars, and technological advances. Each kingdom develops a unique history through its Chronicle.

**Source**: Handoff, Chronicle section; Genesis, The Chronicle section

---

### Colony

A secondary settlement beyond the capital. Multiple colonies may exist. Each colony develops its own specialization and identity — not through an explicit menu, but naturally from its buildings, geography, production, and history.

**Source**: Handoff, Colonies section; Genesis, Colonies section

---

### Cozy Complexity

One of the six design pillars. Rich systems with low stress. No unnecessary micromanagement. Depth through interaction rather than punishment.

**Source**: Handoff, Core Design Philosophy section

---

### Desire Path

A dirt path that villagers naturally create by repeatedly walking between important locations. These paths become visible over time. The player can pave them into roads. This mechanic illustrates the project philosophy: the simulation teaches the player, and the player improves the simulation.

**Source**: Handoff, Roads section; Genesis, Roads and Desire Paths section

---

### District

A planned area within a settlement. Players can designate districts as part of grid-based construction and settlement planning.

**Source**: Handoff, Settlement section (implied by "districts" as a buildable element)

---

### Emergent Stories

One of the six design pillars. The best stories should come from simulation rather than scripted events. The Chronicle and Living Memories systems are the primary vehicles for emergent storytelling.

**Source**: Handoff, Core Design Philosophy section

---

### Hero

A named character with personality, backstory, and unique abilities. Approximately 10–20 heroes exist in the game. Heroes coexist with ordinary villagers. They are long-lived members of society, not disposable units. Progression focuses primarily on levels and professions. Hero mortality depends on difficulty setting (Easy: never die; Medium: injured, return to capital; Hard: permanent death possible).

**Source**: Handoff, Heroes section; Genesis, Heroes section

---

### Household

A family unit formed by villagers. Villagers move into the kingdom, form households, marry, and have children. Households are the basic social unit of the simulation.

**Source**: Handoff, Villagers section

---

### Living Memory

A biography system for heroes and important villagers. Records birthplace, family, profession, achievements, expeditions, and legacy. The goal is emotional attachment rather than purely statistical tracking.

**Source**: Handoff, Living Memories section; Genesis, Living Memories section

---

### Living Simulation

One of the six design pillars. The world exists independently of the player. Villagers have lives. Heroes become legends. Settlements develop identities. History emerges naturally. Instead of buildings merely producing resources, the kingdom should feel inhabited.

**Source**: Handoff, Core Design Philosophy section; Genesis, Living Simulation section

---

### Meaningful Growth

One of the six design pillars. Progress should always be visible. Research changes architecture. Roads improve logistics. Villages become cities. Children become adults. The kingdom physically evolves.

**Source**: Handoff, Core Design Philosophy section

---

### North Star

The guiding design heuristic: "Make the kingdom feel more alive." Not more complicated. Not more difficult. More alive. Whenever uncertainty exists, this principle should outweigh individual mechanics.

**Source**: Handoff, The North Star section; Genesis, The North Star section

---

### Player Expression

One of the six design pillars. Players should have extensive freedom: grid-based construction, road placement, district planning, decorating, colonies. No two kingdoms should look alike. Decorations are meaningful — they influence happiness, prestige, and settlement quality.

**Source**: Handoff, Core Design Philosophy section

---

### Respect the Player's Time

One of the six design pillars and perhaps the most defining principle. The game will never include daily rewards, battle passes, energy systems, premium currency, daily quests, FOMO mechanics, login streaks, or mechanics that punish taking breaks. The kingdom patiently waits. The player returns because they want to.

**Source**: Handoff, Core Design Philosophy section; Genesis, Respect the Player's Time section

---

### Settlement

The general term for the player's built environment — encompassing the capital and all colonies. The settlement (not combat) is the heart of the game. The settlement is considered the true protagonist of the project.

**Source**: Handoff, Gameplay Vision section; Genesis, The Settlement Becomes the Main Character section

---

### Six-Month Return Test

A design heuristic: "Will this make the player smile when returning after six months?" Every feature should be evaluated against this question. If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.

**Source**: Handoff, The Six-Month Return Test section; Genesis, The Six-Month Return Test section

---

### Trade Route

A connection between settlements or regions through which resources flow. Roads improve trade routes. Resources physically move — nothing teleports.

**Source**: Handoff, Roads section; Glossary section

---

### Villager

A simulated individual living in the kingdom. Villagers move in, form households, marry, have children, age, attend school, learn professions, work, retire, and die. Unlike heroes, villagers are procedurally generated and numerous.

**Source**: Handoff, Villagers section; Genesis, Living Simulation section

---

### Visible Logistics

The principle that nothing teleports. Resources physically move through the kingdom: Mine → Cart → Warehouse → Blacksmith → Market. Improving logistics should produce visible changes the player can watch.

**Source**: Handoff, Visible Logistics section; Genesis, Visible Logistics section
